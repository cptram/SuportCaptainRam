Mission Brief
Why The Academy Exists
"The First Fleet learned everything the hard way. The Second Fleet Academy exists so you don't have to."

Founded in the deep lanes between systems, RAM Space Industries grew from a small crew of contractors bound by one conviction: that the right team, given the right mission, has no ceiling.

Every hard lesson from those early years — every ambush, every failed cargo run, every callout that came too late — is now a training module in the Academy. What once cost ships and credits now costs a few hours in orientation.

When you graduate, you don't just join an org. You join a second fleet built on everything the first one learned.

Core Values
R

Reliability

M

Know your role. Whatever position you hold on a ship, be the best at it you can be. Expertise saves lives — and cargo.

Welcome to RAM Space Industries

Welcome, Cadet. You have taken the first step toward joining RAM Space Industries — one of the verse's most dedicated multi-role organizations. Before you earn your division assignment and your crew rank, you need to understand who we are, how we operate, and what we expect of every person who flies under the RAMSORG tag.

Who We Are

RAM Space Industries was founded on a simple idea: that the right crew, given the right mission, has no ceiling. We are lawful. We are role-play friendly. We run cargo, we run security, we explore, we mine, we build. We do not pick one lane and ignore the rest — we build for every kind of player.

The RAM Doctrine

  • Reliability — If you sign up, you show up. Your crew plans around you.
  • Adaptability — The verse changes with every patch. Stay flexible, stay effective.
  • Mastery — Know your role. Do it well. The crew depends on your competence as much as your effort.
Cadet note: You are joining as a Cadet — this is your rank while you complete the Academy. Once you graduate, you become a Crew Member and receive your official division assignment.
02
Rank Structure & Progression
Unread
▾

RAMSORG uses a nine-rank system across three tiers. Understanding where you sit, how to move up, and what each rank means is essential for knowing who to take direction from and how to earn your place.

The Three Tiers

  • Command Tier — Admiral, Fleet Commander, Captain. Set strategy, run fleet ops, have final say.
  • Officer Tier — Division Head, Lieutenant, Specialist. Experienced members in leadership positions. Specialists are the subject matter experts and mentors.
  • Crew Tier — Senior Crew, Crew Member, Cadet. This is where you start. Crew Members are the backbone of every op.

How You Advance

Rank is earned through Standing Points (SP). Earn SP by attending events, completing ops, recruiting members, and teaching in the Academy. There is no shortcut — but there is a clear path.

Your current rank: Cadet. Next milestone: Crew Member — earned by completing all Academy stages and attending your first live op.

The Four Divisions
Unread

Every member of RAMSORG belongs to a division. Your division is your lane, your crew, and your career track. You will pick your primary division in the Division Select tab — read this first so you can make an informed choice.

SHIELD — Security & Combat

  • Fighter pilots, gunners, marines, and medics who protect the org's assets and personnel.
  • Runs escort missions, bounty hunting, PvP response, and base defense.
  • Best for: players who love combat, dogfighting, or FPS gameplay.

HAUL — Logistics & Cargo

  • Cargo pilots, refuelers, trade analysts, and convoy leads who keep the org funded.
  • Runs freight convoys, trade route optimization, and supply chain missions.
  • Best for: players who prefer trading, hauling, and economic gameplay.

SCOUT — Exploration & Recon

  • Pathfinders, salvagers, data runners, and cartographers who map the verse.
  • Runs deep space surveys, jump point scouting, salvage sweeps, and recon ops.
  • Best for: players who love exploration, discovery, and solo or small-team play.

FORGE — Engineering & Industry

  • Miners, ship engineers, base builders, and repair specialists who build the org's foundation.
  • Runs mining operations, ship repair during fleet ops, and future base construction.
  • Best for: players who enjoy industry, crafting, engineering, and support roles.
Important: Your primary division is your starting point — not your cage. After reaching Crew Member rank, return here to study secondary division modules and cross-train. The verse rewards versatility.
04
Events, Ops & How We Run Them
Unread
▾

RAMSORG runs a structured weekly event schedule. Knowing the event types and how they work is essential before your first live op.

Weekly Rhythm

  • Monday — Open Contracts Night: Informal. Members run missions together. No assigned roles. Great for your first session.
  • Wednesday — Division Ops: Structured. Each division runs its own focused activity. Roles are assigned.
  • Saturday — Fleet Op: The main event. Multi-ship, all divisions working together. Earns the most Standing Points.
  • Monthly — Org Meeting: Voice meeting on Discord. Promotions announced, strategy discussed, next month planned.

How Fleet Ops Work

  • Ops are announced 3+ days ahead in #fleet-ops-announcements.
  • Sign up by reacting with your division emoji or posting your ship and preferred role.
  • Captain assigns positions 24 hours before the op.
  • Everyone joins voice for a muster brief — objective, callsigns, rules of engagement.
  • After the op, your Captain files an after-action report. This is how SP are awarded.
Comms rule: In a live op, keep voice chat clear. Call out threats, statuses, and positions — nothing else until debrief.
05
Standing Points & The Org Economy
Unread
▾

Standing Points (SP) track your contribution and progress toward rank. No shortcuts, but the path is clear.

How You Earn SP

  • Casual event attendance — +1 SP
  • Division Op completion — +2 SP
  • Fleet Op participation — +3 SP
  • MVP / Commendation — +2 SP bonus
  • Recruiting a new member — +3 SP
  • Teaching an Academy module — +4 SP
  • Org contributions — +1–5 SP at leadership discretion

Rank Thresholds

  • 5 SP — eligible for Crew Member
  • 20 SP — eligible for Senior Crew
  • 40 SP — eligible for Specialist
  • 60 SP — eligible for Lieutenant / Division Head

Code of Conduct & Discord
Unread
▾

RAMSORG runs on trust, respect, and Discord. This module covers the rules every member follows and how to use the org's communication structure.

Code of Conduct

  • Treat every member with respect — in ops, in chat, and in voice. Zero tolerance for harassment.
  • No griefing org members or allies. Ever. Disputes go to a Division Head.
  • If you commit to an op, show up. If you can't, notify leadership in advance.
  • Follow your Captain's orders in an op. Save disagreements for the after-action debrief.
  • No unauthorized PvP that puts the org at risk — check rules of engagement before engaging.
